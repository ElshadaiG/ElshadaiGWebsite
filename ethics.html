Introduction

IT ethics refers to the moral principles that guide how technology is developed and used. Professionals must ensure their work benefits society and avoids harm.

ACM Code of Ethics

Based on :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Contribute to Society

Technology should improve people's lives and prioritize the public good.

Avoid Harm

Developers should prevent harm and reduce risks in their systems.

Be Honest

Professionals must be truthful about their work and system capabilities.

Be Fair

Technology should not discriminate and must be accessible to all users.

Respect Privacy

User data must be protected and used responsibly.

IEEE Code of Ethics

Based on :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Protect Public Safety

Always prioritize the safety, health, and well-being of the public.

Be Honest and Ethical

Do not engage in dishonest or illegal activities and always correct mistakes.

Avoid Conflicts of Interest

Be transparent and do not let personal interests affect decisions.

Treat Everyone Fairly

Respect all individuals and avoid discrimination or harassment.

Maintain Professional Skills

Continue learning and only perform tasks you are qualified to do.
